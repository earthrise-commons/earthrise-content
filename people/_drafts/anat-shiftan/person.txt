Bio: <p><strong>Anat Shiftan</strong>, an Israeli artist who works in clay and print, explores the subject of ambivalence in floral and zoological imagery; the textures of sexuality, life and death; and power and subversion, as reflections of complex political and social attitudes. Shiftan received her BA in English Literature and Philosophy from the Hebrew University, Jerusalem and her MFA in Ceramics from the Cranbrook Academy of Art and Design. She taught at Bezalel Academy for Art and Design, University of Michigan, and SUNY New Paltz. Shiftan received the Michigan Grant for Individual Artists and is represented by Hostler Burrows gallery NY/LA.</p>
