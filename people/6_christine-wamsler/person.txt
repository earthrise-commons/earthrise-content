Bio: <p><strong>About&nbsp;Christine</strong></p><p>Christine&nbsp;is Professor of Sustainability Science at Lund University Centre for Sustainability Studies (<u><a href="http://www.lucsus.lu.se/" target="_blank" title="http://www.lucsus.lu.se/">LUCSUS</a></u>), Founder and Director of the&nbsp;<u><a href="http://www.contemplative-sustainable-futures.com/" target="_blank" title="http://www.contemplative-sustainable-futures.com/">Contemplative Sustainable Futures Program</a></u>, and former Co-Director of the Societal Resilience Centre. She is an internationally-renowned expert in sustainable development and associated (material and cognitive) transformation processes, with 25 years of experience, both in theory and practice.&nbsp;</p><p>Christine&nbsp;has been ranked as the world’s eighth most influential scholar in the field of environmental science.&nbsp;</p><p>She is leading the emergent field of inner transformation, mindfulness, and sustainability, and has co-developed related theories, approaches, leadership, and educational programs for climate action and sustainability.&nbsp;</p><p>Her work has shaped international debates and increased knowledge on personal, collective, institutional, and policy transformations in a context of global environmental change. She has led many international projects and published more than 200 academic papers, book chapters, and books on these issues. </p><p>Her publications are regularly cited and used in theory and policy development, including by the Intergovernmental Panel on Climate Change (IPCC) and the Intergovernmental Science-Policy Platform on Biodiversity and Ecosystem Services (IPBES).&nbsp;Christine&nbsp;serves as a senior scientific advisor for the UNDP Conscious Food Systems Alliance, the Global Compassion Coalition, the Inner Green Deal, the Inner Development Goals (IDGs) Initiative, and the Science Centre (Science Village).</p><p>Her workplace, LUCSUS, is a pioneer in transdisciplinary research and collaboration, combining critical perspectives with
